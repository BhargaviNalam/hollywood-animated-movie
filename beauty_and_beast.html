The Story : The Beauty And The Beast

The story begins with Belle, a bright and independent young woman who feels out of place in her small village. Her father, Maurice, is an inventor who gets lost in the woods and stumbles upon a mysterious castle. Captured by the Beast, a once-prince cursed for his selfishness, Maurice's plight leads Belle to offer herself in exchange for her father's freedom. As Belle settles into the castle, she discovers its enchanted inhabitants, including Lumière, Cogsworth, and Mrs. Potts, who help her navigate life in the castle. Initially frightened by the Beast, Belle gradually sees past his fearsome exterior and learns about the loneliness and pain that drives him. Meanwhile, Gaston, a local hunter, is determined to win Belle’s affections, believing she should be with him rather than the Beast. His arrogance and cruelty stand in stark contrast to the growing bond between Belle and the Beast. Through their interactions, Belle teaches the Beast about love and compassion, and he begins to change. However, Gaston's jealousy and anger threaten their happiness. Ultimately, the story culminates in a battle between Gaston and the Beast, leading to a dramatic confrontation. In the end, Belle’s love for the Beast breaks the curse, transforming him back into a prince. The story concludes with the celebration of their love and the restoration of the castle and its enchanted servants, emphasizing that true beauty lies within.
